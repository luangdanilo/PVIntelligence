Medical Products including Vaccines - Responsible Pharmacovigilance Person / Pharmacovigilance System Master File (PSMF) Requirements

Regional Responsible Person in Pharmacovigilance = Contact person overseeing the Marketing Authorization Holder PV system across the countries of a region where the product is approved (e.g Qualified Person for Pharmacovigilance-QPPV in EU) under regional and/or national law
Responsible person in Pharmacovigilance (RPP) = Contact person overseeing the Marketing Authorization Holder PV system from the country where the product is approved (e.g. QPPV in a country/ PV representative/PV responsible person) under the law of the country

Medicinal Product - Expedited Reporting Rules

ICSR Strongly Regulated Countries (SRC) = Local expedited reporting regulatory requirements for MAH/sponsor. The Reporting rules provide local requirements to ensure further ICSR submission to local/regional Health Authority
ICSR non Strongly Regulated Countries (NSRC) = No local expedited reporting regulatory requirements to HA for MAH/sponsor. The Reporting rules are configured so that Local PV representatives (e.g .CSL), receive, for information only, CIOMS-I form of local ICSR.

  • EEA = From any EEA Member States (i.e. 28 Member States of the European Union + Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway)
  • exEEA = Outside the EEA
  • EEU = EurAsia, China & JPACn Economic Union (=Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Russia)
  • exEEU = Outside the EEU
1ICSR collection type could be
- Unsolicited - refers to ADR originating from unsolicited source such as spontaneous notification, digital media, literature and prospective or retrospective disease registries or cohort.
- Solicited - refers to AE originating from solicited source such as:
  • Interventional clinical trials and compassionate use with a non-registered product: Solicited (CT)
  • Post-authorization non interventional clinical study, compassionate use (with a registered product), prospective or retrospective product registries or cohort with a primary data collection form, patient support programs, named patient programs and market research programs: Solicited (PM)
2Submission Rule : The ICSR reporting to Regulatory Authority follows one of the following rules and depends on the ICSR collection:
- CT Rule : interventional clinical trials reporting legislation
- PM Rule: post marketing PV legislation
3Related criteria in the unsolicited reporting rules: any report notified spontaneously is considered as a reaction, ie related to the medicinal product
4Unexpectedness criteria :
- When Unexpected ICSR should be submitted as per local SPC, all ISCR will be released to the affiliate so that the listedness is done as per local SPC and not as per CCSI.
- When Unexpected ICSR should be submitted as per local CCSI, only unexpected ISCR will be released to the affiliate.
5Medically Confirmed : applicable only for unsolicited RR, since all solicited ICSR are reported by investigators.
6Timeslines - if only days specified, should be considered as calendar days. day = cd
-PM submission rule :e.g 15 days/ 90 days timelines = 15 day for serious / 90 days for non serious
- CT submission rule : e.g. 7 days/ 15 days timelines = 7 day for D/LT / 15 days for other seriouness criteria
- Other timelines clarified in the comment section.

SUMMARY OF RESPONSIBILITY PER COUNTRY FOR THE SAFETY REPORTING OBLIGATIONS
IN A INTERVENTIONAL CLINICAL TRIAL (EXPEDITED ICSRs and PERIODIC CLINICAL STUDY SAFETY REPORT)

The summary of Responsibility per Country for the Safety Reporting Obligations has been also established when a CRO has been subcontracted in a clinical trial.

For any update in a regulation or sharing of responsibility during a clinical trial, the CSH, Clinical Safety Unit Safety Lead or equivalent should inform:

  • GPV QPPV office - Safety Regulatory Intelligence to update the table
  • CRO to update the contract
ICSR = Individual Case Safety Report
SUSAR = Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction
SSAR = Semi Annual Safety Report
DSUR = Development Safety Update Report
In case of sharing of responsibility with CRO, it might that specific interface should be implemented locally due to the national regulatory requirement. The below option can be chosen for a safety report:
(a) CRO will forward Sanofi country of a copy of AE/ SAE form from the country. So that Sanofi country will be able to prepare and submit the expedited/line-listing/periodic ICSR to Health Authority (HA).
(b) CRO will inform Sanofi country of a traceability of SUSAR/ Periodic Report submitted to Ethic Committee (EC)
(c) Sanofi country will inform/submit CRO of a traceability of ICSR - Expedited and Periodic Report submitted to HA.
(d) Sanofi country will inform CRO of ICSR to be submitted to EC
(e) CRO will inform Sanofi country of a traceability of safety report (e.g. SUSAR, DSUR and Quarterly) submitted to HA, and when required Sanofi country will forward CRO a copy of required CIOMS forms
